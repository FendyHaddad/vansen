Acceptable Use Policy

Last updated: 17 July 2026

This policy sets the rules for what you may create, upload, and generate on Vansen. It forms part of our Terms of Service, and it applies to your prompts, uploads, edits, and outputs.

  1. 1. Purpose
  2. 2. Zero-tolerance content
  3. 3. Prohibited content and conduct
  4. 4. Provider rules
  5. 5. Enforcement
  6. 6. Appeals
  7. 7. Reporting

1. Purpose

We want Vansen to be safe, lawful, and usable by everyone. This Acceptable Use Policy describes content and conduct that are not allowed. It is incorporated into the Terms of Service, and by using the Service you agree to follow it. Where a term is defined in the Terms, it has the same meaning here.

2. Zero-tolerance content

We have zero tolerance for content that sexualises minors. You may not create, upload, request, or distribute:

  • any sexual content involving a minor, or any person or character depicted, described, or presented as a minor, whether real or generated; or
  • any content that otherwise sexualises minors.

Violations in this category are not subject to the two-strike process below. They result in immediate account termination, preservation of evidence, and reporting to law enforcement or the relevant authorities where we are required or permitted to do so.

3. Prohibited content and conduct

You may not use the Service to create, upload, request, distribute, or engage in:

  • content or activity that is illegal under any law that applies to you;
  • non-consensual intimate imagery, including sexual or nude depictions of real, identifiable people created without their consent ("deepfake" intimate imagery);
  • impersonation of a real person, or fabricated statements or events presented as real, in a deceptive or harmful way;
  • harassment, bullying, threats, hateful content, or content that incites or promotes violence against people or groups;
  • promotion or facilitation of terrorism or violent extremism;
  • promotion or encouragement of self-harm or suicide;
  • content that provides operational uplift for weapons, including chemical, biological, radiological, or nuclear (CBRN) harm;
  • fraud, scams, phishing, deceptive schemes, or spam;
  • malware, or attempts to attack, disrupt, or abuse the Service — including probing or overloading the API, evading or defeating moderation, reverse-engineering our rate limits or credit system, sharing credentials, or scraping;
  • infringement or violation of others' intellectual-property, publicity, or privacy rights;
  • synthetic or manipulated media presented without the disclosure that applicable law requires (for example, transparency obligations under the EU AI Act); or
  • circumventing a suspension, ban, or our age-eligibility gate.

4. Provider rules

Generations run on third-party models are also subject to the usage policies of the provider of that model, including those of Google, OpenAI, and fal.ai. Those provider rules apply in addition to this policy, and you agree to comply with them when you use the corresponding models.

5. Enforcement

Content is screened by automated moderation before any charge is made and before any request is sent to a provider. When a violation is detected, we log it together with the relevant evidence. For most violations we operate a two strikes policy: a second violation results in suspension of your account. The zero-tolerance categories in section 2 skip the strike process and lead to immediate termination.

Where lawful, we may remove content, suspend or terminate accounts, report to authorities, and withhold remaining credits in cases of material breach.

6. Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was a mistake, you may appeal by emailing support@vankode.com within 30 days. A person will review your appeal, and we retain the relevant evidence for the appeal window so the decision can be reconsidered.

7. Reporting

If you encounter content on the Service that violates this policy, please report it to support@vankode.com so we can act on it.
